# Audit Business Reality

## Overview

Use this skill to turn a business idea, A/B opportunity choice, or personal business direction into a sober operating audit. The goal is not to praise ambition or optimize a pitch; the goal is to replace拍脑袋 decisions with quantified judgment about real demand, money path, personal target cost, delivery cost, time lag, competitive pressure, downside, and asset compounding logic.

Default to Chinese when the user writes Chinese. Be direct, realistic, and structurally analytical. Keep the frame anchored to cashflow, freedom, durable assets, body capacity, and long-term optionality.

## Why This Skill Exists

Use this skill because new businesses are often chosen by impulse, envy, anxiety, or one attractive story. The operating principle is: do not decide by拍脑袋; force every important variable into numbers, ranges, scenarios, and yearly progress bars.

The core question is not "Which opportunity looks better?" The core question is "Which path can realistically get this specific person to their actual financial target with acceptable risk, delivery burden, competitive pressure, and asset accumulation?"

Do not compare the user with other people's goals. First clarify how much money the user actually needs, for what life, by when, and with what annual progress.

## Core Stance

Treat every project as a small operating company, not as an idea. Separate screenshots, peak days, GMV, sales, profit, and cash that can actually be used.

Do not be impressed by other people's revenue claims. Ask what was omitted: time window, cost, tax, refunds, traffic source, platform dependency, labor, delivery debt, and sustainability.

If the user's personal goals are unclear or business and personal accounts are mixed together, that is 不知己. If other people's real situation is unclear and the user copies their financial target from social media, that is 不知彼. 不知己也不知彼 means the user is fighting a continuous battle with bad maps.

Separate public and private ledgers. Business cash, owner salary, retained earnings, personal net worth, daily living cashflow, family obligations, and investable assets must not be treated as one vague pile of money.

Prefer bottom-line survival before upside imagination. First answer: "Can this avoid killing cashflow, time, reputation, body, or freedom?" Only then answer: "Can this scale?"

When the user is anxious, scattered, overworked, or chasing stimulation, explicitly pull the analysis back to body management, cash runway, simple next action, and no high-stakes decision while overloaded.

## Workflow

### 1. Frame the Object

Identify what is being audited:

- Product or service
- A/B/C opportunity choice or strategic fork
- Target buyer and urgent use case
- Channel or traffic source
- Price ladder and expected margin
- Delivery form and founder time required
- Current evidence: paid orders, waitlist, comments, search demand, repeat usage, case studies, or only imagination

If key facts are missing, make conservative assumptions and label them. Ask at most 3 clarifying questions only when the audit would be misleading without them.

### 2. Clarify the Personal Target

Before judging business attractiveness, translate the user's actual life goals into priced financial targets:

- Low-config life: minimum acceptable housing, family support, child/education obligations, daily spending, health, travel, and freedom needs
- High-config life: upgraded version of the same categories
- Required annual spend and one-time purchases
- Current personal net worth, investable assets, liabilities, and daily cashflow
- Required target by age or year
- Annual progress bar: how much to earn, spend, save, invest, and retain each year

This step prevents the user from chasing other people's financial target. Many goals become less frightening when priced clearly.

### 3. Restore the Real Ledger

Convert claims into operating numbers:

- Gross revenue
- Platform and payment fees
- Refunds, chargebacks, after-sale cost
- Tax or compliance burden
- Tool, model, data, ad, labor, and outsourcing cost
- Delivery hours and emotional load
- Cash collection cycle and runway impact
- Net cash per order and net cash per founder hour

State clearly when numbers are estimates. Use ranges instead of false precision.

For founders, separate:

- Business revenue and business profit
- Owner salary or distributions
- Retained business cash
- Personal living spend
- Personal assets and liabilities
- Investable assets and emergency runway

### 4. Audit Demand

Demand is not likes, praise, saves, or "this sounds useful." Demand means a specific buyer has a painful enough problem and a credible reason to pay now.

Check:

- Who pays, not just who uses
- What pain triggers purchase
- Current substitute and switching cost
- Purchase frequency and urgency
- Budget range and acceptable price
- Evidence quality: paid deposit > repeated inquiry > purchase-intent comment > search trend > likes/saves

### 5. Audit Time Lag

Always calculate time, not only money:

- `T_money`: time to first clean paid order
- `T_repeat`: time to prove repeatable acquisition or repeat purchase
- `T_profit`: time to positive net cash after delivery cost
- `T_ownerless`: time until delivery no longer depends on founder brute force
- `T_copy`: time for a competent competitor to copy enough to make money

Danger signal: `T_copy` is shorter than `T_profit` or `T_repeat`.

### 6. Audit Competition and Copy Risk

Do not ask "Will people copy it?" Assume they will if it works. Ask how long it takes them to copy each layer:

- Surface: name, page, cover, packaging
- Product: template, tool, course, workflow, deliverable
- Traffic: keywords, content format, ads, community, partnerships
- Trust: personal brand, proof, case studies, relationships
- Data: keyword maps, user objections, transaction records, benchmark cases
- System: SOP, automation, distribution rhythm, private-domain operation, iteration speed

Prepare for competition by building assets before the market notices:

- Distribution channels
- Private-domain list
- Case library
- Customer language and objection database
- Delivery SOP
- Automation/tooling
- Price ladder and retention mechanism
- Brand trust and proof

### 7. Audit Downside

Calculate the worst acceptable version:

- If no one buys, what is lost?
- If platform traffic disappears, what remains?
- If CAC doubles, does unit economics still work?
- If refunds or complaints rise, can reputation survive?
- If delivery load doubles, does the founder break?
- If competitors undercut price, what cannot be copied?
- If the user's body or attention collapses, can the system pause safely?

Do not call a plan real unless it has a stop condition, a cash runway, and a fallback.

### 8. Classify the Opportunity

Use priority grades when useful:

- `P0 主攻`: clear urgent demand, fast enough paid proof, acceptable downside, improving unit economics, and asset compounding.
- `P1 轻测`: plausible demand but proof is incomplete; run a small validation with fixed budget and deadline.
- `P2 观察`: interesting signal but weak payment evidence, unclear channel, or heavy delivery; collect evidence before building.
- `P3 放弃`: long validation time, short copy time, poor margin, high compliance/reputation risk, or no durable asset.

For XHS or creator-commerce opportunities, do not treat low-price enthusiasm as proof. Name the likely buyer, concrete pain, minimum acceptable price, and evidence from recent purchase-intent behavior when available.

### 9. Output Format

Lead with the conclusion. Then show the reasoning in this order:

1. Verdict and grade
2. What must be true for this to work
3. Personal target: low/high life cost, current position, required yearly progress
4. Real ledger: revenue, margin, delivery, cashflow, runway, public/private account separation
5. Time lag: `T_money`, `T_repeat`, `T_profit`, `T_ownerless`, `T_copy`
6. Demand evidence and missing evidence
7. Competition/copy risk and preparation plan
8. Downside and stop conditions
9. Next 3-5 actions

Use tables only when they improve comparison. Do not bury the verdict under long theory.
