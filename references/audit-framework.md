# Audit Framework

Use this reference when the user wants a complete business reality audit, a scorecard, or a reusable evaluation template.

## Foundational Logic

This skill exists to prevent business choices from being made by impulse, envy, social-media comparison, or a single exciting story. New business decisions should be made with quantified information:

- What does the user actually need to earn?
- What does each life goal cost?
- What is the annual progress bar?
- What does each business path produce in cash, risk, time, and durable assets?
- How long does the user need to run before the model works?
- How long does it take others to copy the model?

If personal goals are unclear or business and personal accounts are mixed together, the user does not know themselves. If competitors' or peers' real financial situation is unclear and the user treats their displayed numbers as a target, the user does not know the other side. The audit must repair both.

## Personal Target Ledger

Before choosing A/B opportunities, translate life goals into priced financial targets. Do not let the user borrow other people's target from social media.

| Category | Low-config version | High-config version | Pricing question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Housing | Minimum acceptable city/area/rent/buy plan | Preferred city/area/ownership plan | What is the down payment, mortgage/rent, tax, maintenance? |
| Family support | Basic family obligations | More generous support | Annual fixed amount and emergency reserve? |
| Child/education | Required childcare/schooling | Preferred education path | Annual cost and one-time spikes? |
| Daily spending | Stable, non-humiliating baseline | Comfortable upgrade | Monthly spend after removing vanity? |
| Health/body | Minimum healthcare, exercise, recovery | Stronger health infrastructure | Annual spend and time budget? |
| Travel/experience | A few realistic trips | Frequent higher-quality trips | Annual cash cost and time cost? |
| Freedom buffer | Emergency runway | Strong optionality buffer | Months of living cost covered? |

Then produce:

- Current personal net worth
- Liquid cash and emergency runway
- Investable assets
- Liabilities and mandatory payments
- Annual personal spending
- Annual target saving/investing
- Target age/year for low-config and high-config life

The point is often emotional as much as financial: once the goal has a price, the user may see that the target is reachable if they avoid major mistakes.

## Public/Private Ledger Separation

Do not mix company money and personal money.

| Ledger | Include | Do not confuse with |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Business revenue | Sales, collected cash, receivables | Owner income |
| Business profit | Revenue after real business costs | Free personal cash |
| Retained business cash | Money kept for inventory, payroll, ads, tax, runway | Spendable personal wealth |
| Owner salary/distribution | Cash actually transferred to the owner | Gross revenue screenshots |
| Personal net worth | Personal assets minus personal liabilities | Company top-line numbers |
| Personal cashflow | Living spend, family obligations, debt payments | Business operating cash |
| Investable assets | Assets that can realistically be deployed | Locked assets or emotional assets |

If this separation is impossible from current data, the first recommendation should be to build the ledger before making large strategic decisions.

## Annual Progress Bar

Convert financial goals into a year-by-year route:

| Year/Age | Business net cash | Owner income | Personal spend | Savings/investment | Investment return | Net worth target | Gap |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Y1 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| Y2 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| Y3 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

For each A/B path, compare whether the path can support the annual progress bar without requiring heroic assumptions.

Use conservative scenarios:

- Base case: realistic current ability and current channel
- Bad case: slower sales, higher cost, platform or competitor pressure
- Good case: growth after proof, not fantasy before proof

## Evidence Quality Ladder

Rank evidence from strongest to weakest:

1. Repeated paid orders from the target buyer
2. Paid deposit, preorder, renewal, or referral
3. High-intent sales conversation with price acceptance
4. Search demand plus clear buyer pain
5. Purchase-intent comments from recent hot content
6. Saves, likes, praise, curiosity, or "I would use this"
7. Founder intuition without market contact

Do not let weak evidence support a strong recommendation.

## Real Ledger Template

Ask for or estimate:

| Item | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Price | What does one customer pay? Is there a ladder? |
| Gross revenue | Is this GMV, booked sales, or collected cash? |
| Variable cost | Payment fee, platform fee, model cost, materials, labor, fulfillment |
| Refund/after-sale | What share returns, complains, or needs support? |
| Acquisition cost | Paid ads, content time, affiliate cut, community cost |
| Delivery time | Founder hours per order and per customer lifecycle |
| Cash cycle | When is cash collected, held, refunded, or paid out? |
| Net cash | What remains after real cost and realistic tax/compliance reserve? |
| Founder hourly yield | Net cash divided by founder hours |

If the founder hourly yield is low and no durable asset is created, classify it as labor, not leverage.

## Time-Lag Test

Use these variables:

| Variable | Meaning | Good Signal | Bad Signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `T_money` | Time to first clean paid order | Days or weeks | Months of building before payment |
| `T_repeat` | Time to repeatable acquisition or repeat purchase | Small loop proven | Every order requires heroic effort |
| `T_profit` | Time to net cash after delivery | Soon after first sales | Revenue grows while cash burns |
| `T_ownerless` | Time to lower founder dependency | SOP/tool/team possible | Founder remains bottleneck forever |
| `T_copy` | Competitor time to copy enough to profit | Longer than proof cycle | Shorter than proof cycle |
| `T_moat` | Time to build durable advantage | Starts immediately | Begins only after scale fantasy |

Decision rule:

- `T_money` short and `T_copy` long: strong candidate.
- `T_money` short and `T_copy` short: cashflow play; move fast and harvest, but do not worship it.
- `T_money` long and `T_copy` long: asset play; only pursue with runway.
- `T_money` long and `T_copy` short: usually reject.

## Copy Risk Layers

Audit what competitors can and cannot copy:

| Layer | Easy to copy | Harder to copy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Surface | Name, page, cover, offer wording | Distinct positioning with proof |
| Product | Template, checklist, prompt pack | Workflow quality, domain judgment, update rhythm |
| Channel | Visible posts, keywords, ads | Account trust, private domain, partnerships |
| Proof | Screenshots | Real cases, before/after, longitudinal outcomes |
| Data | Public comments | Proprietary user language, objection bank, transaction data |
| System | Manual steps | Automation, SOP, QA, delivery engine |
| Brand | Style mimicry | Reputation, trust, repeated buyer relationship |

If everything valuable is in the first column, classify the project as short-cycle cashflow, not a durable business.

## Competition Readiness Plan

Before competitors arrive, push effort into:

1. Distribution: own channels, search terms, content cadence, private domain.
2. Proof: customer cases, testimonials, measurable outcomes, screenshots with context.
3. Process: SOP, templates, QA checklist, onboarding, delivery automation.
4. Data: keyword map, user pain library, objections, price sensitivity, refund reasons.
5. Product ladder: entry offer, core product, higher-ticket help, renewal or update plan.
6. Trust: founder point of view, consistent language, public methodology.
7. Exit options: stop condition, pivot path, asset reuse path.

## Risk Map

Cover at least these risks:

| Risk | What to ask |
| --- | --- |
| Demand risk | Is payment evidence real enough? |
| Channel risk | What if the main channel stops working? |
| Unit economics risk | What happens when CAC, refund, or support rises? |
| Delivery risk | Does growth increase founder exhaustion? |
| Compliance risk | Any legal, privacy, copyright, platform, or financial-advice boundary? |
| Reputation risk | What promise could backfire publicly? |
| Copy risk | Can a faster player copy and undercut before proof? |
| Body risk | Can the founder execute without sleep, movement, and nervous-system stability collapsing? |

High body risk is business risk. Do not treat it as lifestyle trivia.

## Scorecard

Score 1-5. Use conservative scoring when evidence is weak.

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Buyer pain | Vague curiosity | Clear inconvenience | Urgent costly pain |
| Payment proof | None | Inquiry/deposit | Repeated paid orders |
| Channel access | Unknown | One plausible channel | Proven repeatable acquisition |
| Margin | Low or unclear | Acceptable with founder labor | Strong after real costs |
| Delivery leverage | Manual and draining | Partly templated | SOP/tool/team leverage |
| Time to cash | Long | Moderate | Fast |
| Copy resistance | Surface only | Some process/data | Trust/data/system advantage |
| Asset compounding | None | Some reusable assets | Every cycle improves moat |
| Downside control | Undefined | Limited budget | Clear stop loss and fallback |

Suggested grade:

- Average 4.2+ with no fatal risk: `P0 主攻`
- Average 3.4-4.1 or one major unknown: `P1 轻测`
- Average 2.6-3.3 with weak proof: `P2 观察`
- Below 2.6 or any fatal risk: `P3 放弃`

Fatal risks override averages.
